Making it a habit to create every day allows me to constantly improve as a creator. I practice bringing new ideas to life through drawing and coding, learning new techniques and developing myself along the way. Using these skills, I want to create websites where people have friendly interactions and delight in their experience.

365 Days of Drawing

Inspired by the sense of space and emotion portrayed in concept art, I wanted to learn to paint my own. I set up an Instagram account and posted every day for one year to share my progress with others. I met many friendly and talented people along the way who helped to keep me motivated and on the right track.

Coding Everyday

After I began to see progress with drawing, I added coding to my everyday schedule. I wanted a place to experiment with and create all of the ideas floating around in my head. Since the inception, I've created a small game with level editor, a website to hold some comics that I never wrote, and my current portfolio site. I learned a lot about Phaser, Gulp, Angular, and setting up my own site with DeployBot and NGINX.

Moving Forward

At first doing both coding and drawing every day wasn’t bad, but as days went by the quality of one or both began to suffer. Even though I was learning and progressing my focus began to meander, and I felt I wasn’t learning as much as I could. I’ve since revised my schedule to only work on either one or the other in a single day in order to stay focused. I still do something to improve myself every day with the goal of developing as an artist, designer, and developer.
